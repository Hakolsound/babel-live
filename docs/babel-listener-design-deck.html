01 — Overview

What is Babel Listener?

A dedicated wireless translation receiver deployed at live events — conferences, houses of worship, medical briefings.

Attendees pick it up at the door, power it on, select a language on the touchscreen, and hear a real-time translated audio feed through earbuds or Bluetooth neckloop.

No phone
No app, no setup, no account
< 2 sec
Power-on to stream-ready
400 units
Max fleet per event

Fleet Model

  • 1
    Babel owns and manages the fleet
  • 2
    Staff provisions all devices via single BLE broadcast before event
  • 3
    Attendees pick up a device — no instruction needed
  • 4
    Devices returned and drop-charged in rack case overnight
  • 5
    Staff sweeps room with tablet app to detect missing units

03 — Design Goals

Design Principles

⚡ Zero-Friction Pickup
Attendees need zero instruction. Device on → stream ready in 2 seconds. No WiFi prompt, no app, no login.
🔒 Durability
Fleet devices handled by hundreds of people per month. No exposed fragile parts. Tempered glass lens, no protruding connectors.
🔌 Idiot-Proof Charging
Drop into charging slot → pogo pins contact rail → charges. No cable hunting in the dark after a long event.
✋ Single-Hand Use
Held in one hand while seated. Two side/bottom buttons reachable by thumb. Portrait grip for both. Pro auto-rotates to landscape only for caption reading.
👁 Visual Charge Status
Staff verifies charging state per device from a distance without touching. LED light pipe exits at top — visible from rack aisle.
🔧 Serviceable
Clamshell screws (Torx T6). Battery and peripheral PCB replaceable in 3 minutes. Main board swappable without tools once PCB removed.

Housing
PC/ABS clamshell · 4× M2 Torx T6 · Tempered glass lens (OCA bonded) · Belt clip rail · Lanyard eyelet

Why a separate peripheral PCB?

Main board stays stock
No modification to Waveshare board. Swap a failed main board in the field without rework.
Port positions are free
Jack, buttons, pogo pins placed exactly where the housing needs them — not dictated by the main board layout.
TPA6130A2 on board
No ready-made breakout exists. Custom PCB solves this — chip + 4 caps on the same board.
JLCPCB-assembled
PCM5102A, TPA6130A2, LIS3DH, passives pre-assembled. Hand-solder: jack, buttons, pogo, USB-C, ZIF.

How it works

Device slides into case slot vertically. Gravity + slot walls align the pogo pins to the case spring contacts. No cable, no connector, no orientation to get wrong. Charge begins automatically.

Staff workflow

  • 1
    Drop device into slot
  • 2
    Red LED lights at top — charging confirmed
  • 3
    Green LED overnight — fully charged
  • 4
    400 units charged from standard power strip
USB-C remains accessible in slot for firmware updates while devices charge.
11 — Decisions

Design Decisions — All Resolved

D1
Power button
✓Expose main board button via housing cutout + TPU cap bridge. Fallback: replicate on PCB Rev B if alignment fails in prototype.
D2
Pro button placement
✓Right side in portrait (primary hold) = bottom edge in landscape (captions only). Thumb-reachable in both. Confirm on foam mock-up before PCB lock.
D3
Board connector
✓FFC/FPC · ZIF on both boards · 0.5mm pitch · lower profile than pin header · mechanical standoffs for alignment.
D4
Housing assembly
✓Clamshell · 4× M2 Torx T6 tamper-resistant · battery + PCB accessible after screw removal · fleet-serviceable.
D5
Display lens
✓2mm tempered glass · OCA optical bond · no air gap · both tiers. PCAP touch works through 2mm glass ✓
D6
Belt clip
✓Included, minimal slim glass-filled nylon design. Ships attached to back rail groove. Removable. Lanyard eyelet also included (metal insert, top-left).
